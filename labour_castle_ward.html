Our Priorities for Castle Ward

🏠

Affordable Housing That Works

While the Lib Dems talk, Labour delivers. We've secured hundreds of new affordable homes across Cambridge and will prioritize genuine affordable housing in Castle Ward, not the luxury developments the Lib Dems rubber-stamp.

🚌

Better Public Transport

Labour has fought to protect and improve bus services connecting Darwin Green and Eddington to the city center. We oppose the Lib Dem cuts to local transport that leave residents stranded.

🌳

Green Spaces & Environment

Protecting our local green spaces while ensuring new developments like those around Castle Mound include proper community facilities - something the current Lib Dem councillor has failed to secure.

📢

Transparent Communication

Labour councillors like Simon Smith regularly engage with local media to explain decisions and budget challenges to residents. His public statements on the 'Our Cambridge' transformation programme show the transparent, accountable leadership Castle Ward deserves - in contrast to the silence from our current Lib Dem councillor.

💰

Cost of Living Support

Labour has frozen Council Tax increases while improving services. We understand the pressures facing working families and will continue to provide real support, not empty gestures.

🚴

Safe Cycling & Walking Routes

Labour councillors successfully challenged the County Council's flawed Windsor Road/Oxford Road junction plans that would have blocked cyclists with dangerous barriers. We delivered a better solution with traffic calming that keeps routes open for active travel - while the Lib Dem councillor was nowhere to be seen.

Castle Ward - Fair for All

Affordable Housing Programme - Largest in a Generation

Labour achieved the initial target of 1,000 new homes a year ahead of schedule and is now delivering a minimum of 1,700 new homes including 1,000 council homes - the first Passivhaus accredited council homes in Cambridge. While other councils struggle, Labour delivers.

Community Investment & Support Programme

Over £1 million annually in community grants helping residents in greatest need, funding Citizens' Advice for financial hardship support, running community centres, and investing £120,000 in CCTV upgrades for safer neighborhoods across Cambridge.

Climate Action & Green Investment

Labour has reduced council carbon emissions by 10% through investments in leisure centres with heat pumps and solar panels, while declaring a climate emergency and leading on environmental action across the city.

Transparent Leadership & Public Engagement

Cllr Simon Smith has led public engagement on the council's budget challenges, appearing regularly in media to explain Labour's approach. In recent public statements, he explained how Labour has delivered significant savings through the 'Our Cambridge' transformation programme while taking "a measured approach" to future challenges - demonstrating the transparent, responsible leadership Castle Ward needs.

Financial Management - Protecting Services

Achieved £4.6 million net revenue savings through efficiencies over two years while protecting frontline services and keeping Council Tax increases minimal - committed to saving £6 million by 2026/27 without cutting services residents depend on.

Windsor Road/Oxford Road Junction - Listening to Residents (2024)

When the County Council proposed installing exclusionary barriers that would have blocked cyclists and created dangerous 90-degree turns at the Windsor Road/Oxford Road junction, Labour councillors worked with residents and cycling groups to get the scheme put on hold. The improved solution now includes traffic calming measures without blocking vital cycling routes - showing Labour listens to the community.

Standing Up for Darwin Green Residents (2023)

Cllr Antoinette Nestor organized a major public meeting for Darwin Green residents when Barratt David Wilson announced they would demolish 30 completed houses due to foundation issues. She demanded accountability from developers and fought for residents' rights when the Lib Dem councillor remained silent.
